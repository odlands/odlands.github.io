Strikeplate

The Idea

Strikeplate was born when a member of the team decided that there had to be a better way of navigating the various rooms and floors of the office building where he worked. Key cards were cumbersome to keep track of and did not provide a measurable upgrade to security than a normal key and padlock system. The goal became to turn a device that everyone has (your phone) into a universal key.

The Build

The product consists of two applications both built with Flutter and utilizing Google's Firebase in the back end. Firebase services utilized include Authorization to create and validate users, Firestore to handle our database needs, and Cloud Functions which operate during our room access protocol.

The Web App

Our web application is built solely for the administrator. It allows her to validate new users, add accessibility parameters to any user, and set accessibility options for rooms and buildings.

The Mobile App

Once a user downloads the app and gets validated by their administrator, their phone becomes their key. If she would like to gain access to a room, she opens her app, clicks the lock button in the center of the screen, and holds her phone to the key reader. With the phone communicating with the reader by way of NFC, our room access protocol determines whether or not the user is allowed to gain access to the room.
